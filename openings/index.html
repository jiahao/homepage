Openings

The Julia group at MIT welcomes applications from anyone interested in software development in the Julia language and/or applications to big data, data science, computational science and engineering, numerical analysis, and related fields.

Preference will be given to students with programming experience, especially with Julia. Experience in related languages such as Matlab, Python, R, C/C++, and related projects such as LLVM and MPI is a plus.

Julia Internship Program

The Julia Internship Program offers students worldwide the opportunity to come to MIT and work on open source software developing using Julia or to improve the Julia compiler.

A competitive stipend will be provided to cover travel and local expenses. We thank the Moore Foundation for their generous funding of this internship program.

The duration of the internship is flexible and can be tailored to the needs of individual students.

Who can apply

Applicants must be registered students at any level (K-12 though PhD) and are not limited to MIT students. (MIT students are also eligible to apply through other programs below.)

International students are welcome to apply. Please allow at least four months for visa processing to obtain an appropriate US visa.

How to apply

Please send an application to Dr. Jiahao Chen by email (preferred) or post containing:

  • a cover letter explaining:
  • why the applicant is interested in working for the Julia group
  • which dates the applicant is available
  • what contributions to Julia the applicant has already made (whether completed or in progress)
  • code portfolio, e.g. GitHub account or personal webpage detailing programming projects
  • possible career plans
  • résumé or curriculum vitae (CV)
  • academic transcripts (past grade sheets)

Separately, applicants should arrange to have sent at least one letter of recommendation written by an academic advisor. The letter should not be seen by the applicant.

Some applicants may be asked to complete a programming assignment to assess their proficiency in writing code.

Applications are open now and will be accepted until all positions are filled.

MIT EECS SuperUROPs

MIT EECS undergraduates interested in a SuperUROP (Advanced Undergraduate Research Program) should submit an application through the MIT EECS SuperUROP website.

Applications are currently closed but are expected to open again during the spring semester of 2016.

MIT UROPs

All registered MIT undergraduates in good academic standing, including transfer students, cross-registered Wellesley undergraduates participating through the MIT-Wellesley Exchange Program, and students participating in the Cambridge-MIT Exchange are eligible to apply for research experience through MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP).

The Julia group offers highly competitive pay for paid UROPs commensurate with experience and talent, which may match or even surpass the rate offered through other competitive UROP programs such as the EECS SuperUROP. UROPs are available for academic credit or on a volunteer basis.

How to apply

Please contact Dr. Jiahao Chen and Prof. Alan Edelman to discuss eligibility and available project opportunities before submitting a proposal to the UROP website. Proposals should be 1-3 typed-pages in length and clearly describe the UROP research you plan to conduct.

The MIT UROP office accepts applications several times per year.

The next deadline for summer UROP proposals is Thursday, June 18, 2015.

The deadline for spring and fall semesters is the first Friday of the first week of classes.

MIT PhD students

Prospective students interested in working on a PhD thesis with the Julia group are advised to indicate their interest to Dr. Jiahao Chen and Prof. Alan Edelman in October or November of the year preceding the start date.

We encourage interested students to apply though the Mathematics Department, the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department, the Program in Computational Science and Engineering, or other related graduate program at MIT. Express your interest in the Julia Group's research in your statement.

Deadlines for admission vary, but typically are in December.
